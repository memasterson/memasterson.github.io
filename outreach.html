Astrobites

From 2023-2025, I was a graduate student writer for Astrobites, a collaboration of graduate students that takes scientific articles and condenses them down into bite-size pieces that the public can understand (our target audience being undergraduates). I am also an active member of the Climate Change and Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) Committees.

MIT Graduates Advising Graduate Admissions

I'm an active member of the Grads Advising Graduate Admissions (GAGA) group in the MIT Department of Physics, whose role is to provide the grad student perspective and advice on the admissions process. During the 2024-2025 academic year, I served as one of the co-chairs of GAGA. As part of GAGA, I help run the Physics Graduate Application Assistance Program (PhysGAAP), which aims to provide additional information and help to prospective applications to the MIT Physics PhD program. This program consists of yearly webinars, held in the fall and the recordings posted here, drop-in office hours with current MIT Physics PhD students, and a 1-on-1 mentorship program for students to get individualized help on their application.

MIT Astrogazers

I'm a member of the MIT Sidewalk Astrogazers organization and co-led the organization in 2023-2024. Our group has 5 telescopes, include 3 dobsonians, 1 solar (H$\alpha$), and 1 Unistellar eVscope that we bring to the sidewalks of Cambridge and Boston to show the public! We also partner with local organizations, like the MIT Museum, to host specific events with stargazing.

Astronomy on Tap Boston

I have previously been a member of the organizing committee for Boston's chapter of Astronomy on Tap, which features events at local bars, breweries, and coffee shops where astronomy experts give public talks, followed by space-themed trivia. I've helped host events since our chapter started up again in 2022. Check out our event listings or keep an eye on our social media if you're interested in joining for one of our events!

Volunteering in the Astronomy & Astrophysics Research Lab at the North Carolina Museum of Natural Sciences

During the summers of 2017 and 2019, I was a public outreach volunteer in the Astronomy and Astrophysics Department at the North Carolina Museum of Natural Sciences. While there, I developed and regularly ran a "cart program", where I would teach museum guests about dark matter using visual aids and 3D printed, hands-on tools. I also ran weekly solar observing sessions and supervised a local high school student in the development of another cart program and solar observing.
